What Is Crypto?

Cryptocurrency is a digital/virtual currency that is secured by cryptography which makes it nearly impossible to counterfeit. Most cryptocurrency are decentralized networks based on blockchain technology. Blockchains maintain a secure record of transactions; it collects information together in groups known as “blocks.” Blocks have certain storage capacities, are closed when filled and linked to the previously filled block forming a chain of data known as the “blockchain.” Cryptocurrency is not issued by any central authority, rendering them immune to government interference or manipulation. In short, cryptocurrency is a free market, anyone has the ability to buy, sell or make cryptocurrency. Some of the most notorious cryptocurrency is, Bitcoin, Shiba, Etheruem, and Solana.

NFTs and Crypto

The idea of cryptocurrency was first theorized among scholars and programmers in the 1980s due to the general annoyance with centralized finance. In 1995, David Chaum created the first iteration of crypto with Digicash. While innovative, it failed to gain a large user base. In 1998, Nick Szabo developed Bit Gold which most people consider to be the precursor to bitcoin. It was based on users using their computer power to solve cryptographic puzzles, and receiving a reward; similar to mining bitcoin. In 2008, Satoshi Nakamoto published a paper called “Bitcoin – A Peer to Peer Electronic Cash System”. This was the first idea of a blockchain technology, using proof of work (PoW) verification to secure transactions. He mined the very first block of bitcoin on January 3rd, 2009 with the value being a fraction of a cent. Bitcoin hit $1 in 2011, $1000 in 2013, $19,000 in 2017 and $75,000 today. The second largest cryptocurrency is ethereum, created in 2015 by Vitalik Buterin. It hit $1 within the first year, steadily rising until present day; valued at over $4000.
